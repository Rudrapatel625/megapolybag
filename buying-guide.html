The Ultimate Buyer's Guide: How to Order the Perfect PP Woven Bag

Fact: A 5-rupee packaging mistake can destroy a 500-rupee product.

We often see business owners focusing solely on the "Price per Bag." However, if a bag bursts during transport or your product gets wet due to rain, that "cheap" bag becomes the most expensive mistake you ever made.

Whether you are packing Fertilizers, Chemicals, Rice, or Cement, here is your step-by-step guide to ordering the right specs.

1. Size Matters: Don't Guess the Dimensions

Many buyers order a "50kg bag" without knowing the dimensions. But 50kg of Rice has a different volume than 50kg of Cotton Seed.

The Golden Rule: Calculate the "Empty Margin." After filling your product, you must have at least 4 to 5 inches of empty space at the top for proper stitching or sealing. If the bag is too tight, it will burst when stacked.

2. Strength: Understanding GSM & Denier

This is where most confusion happens. Let's simplify it:

  • GSM (Grams per Square Meter): This is the weight of the fabric. Higher GSM = Thicker fabric.
  • Denier: This is the thickness of the tape used to weave the fabric.

If you are packing a sharp or heavy material (like metal parts or rough stones), ask for High Denier. If you are packing fine powder, ask for High Mesh (tighter weaving) so it doesn't leak.

3. Moisture Protection: Liner vs. Lamination

Does your product absorb water? (e.g., Urea, Sugar, Cement). If yes, a standard woven sack is not enough.

  • Option A (Lamination): A thin plastic layer is stuck to the bag. Good for mild protection and great printing.
  • Option B (LDPE Liner): A loose plastic bag is inserted inside the woven sack. This provides 100% waterproof protection. Essential for fertilizers and chemicals.
