5 Costly Mistakes When Buying Woven Sacks

We often see Purchase Managers focusing only on "Price per Bag." But a cheaper bag can cost you millions in spillage, brand damage, and transit losses. Here is how to buy smart.

1. Ignoring the "Mesh Size" (Denier)

The Mistake: Ordering a standard bag without specifying the mesh density. ✅ The Fix: Ask for a 10x10 or 11x11 mesh if you are packing fine powders (like Chemicals). Standard mesh leaks!

2. Forgetting the "Anti-Skid" Twist

The Mistake: Buying smooth bags that slip off pallets during stacking. ✅ The Fix: Specify "Anti-Skid Weaving" or "Gusseted Bags" for stable stacking in godowns.

3. Choosing Wrong Size (GSM vs. Inches)

The Mistake: Ordering a 50kg bag that is too short, leaving no space for stitching. ✅ The Fix: Always calculate: Product Volume + 15% Empty Space for sealing.

4. Using Non-Laminated Bags for Moisture Goods

Cement, Urea, and Salt absorb moisture from the air. A simple woven sack is not enough. You need BOPP Lamination or an LDPE Liner inside.

5. Not Branding Effectively

Your bag is free advertising. Sending out a plain white bag is a wasted opportunity. BOPP printing costs only marginally more but increases brand recall by 40%.
